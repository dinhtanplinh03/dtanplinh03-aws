On this page, you will present your **Worklog**, which documents your internship journey. Describe **how you completed the internship program**, **how many weeks it took**, and **what tasks you accomplished each week**.

Typically, the internship lasts for **three months**, and the weekly worklog is organized as follows:

**Week 1:** [Getting Started with AWS and Core AWS Services](1.1-week1/)

**Week 2:** [Working on AWS Identity and Access Management (IAM)](1.2-week2/)

**Week 3:** [Working on Amazon S3](1.3-week3/)

**Week 4:** [Working on Amazon RDS](1.4-week4/)

**Week 5:** [Working on Amazon VPC](1.5-week5/)

**Week 6:** [Working on AWS Monitoring and Logging Services](1.6-week6/)

**Week 7:** [Working on Elastic Load Balancing (ELB) and Auto Scaling](1.7-week7/)

**Week 8:** [Working on Docker](1.8-week8/)

**Week 9:** [Working on CI/CD](1.9-week9/)

**Week 10:** [Planning and Designing the AWS Workshop Architecture, Defining the Project Scope and Milestones, and Refining the Solution Architecture Based on Mentor Feedback](1.10-week10/)

**Week 11:** [Implementing an Asynchronous Messaging System Using Amazon SNS and Amazon SQS, and Configuring Amazon CloudWatch for Monitoring and Logging](1.11-week11/)

**Week 12:** [Testing and Validating the Asynchronous Messaging Workflow and Monitoring System, Finalizing the Project, and Preparing for Deployment and the Final Presentation](1.12-Week12/)
